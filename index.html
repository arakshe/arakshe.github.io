Sankey Flow Visualization

Group Members: Anvi Rakshe, Manika Sakulsureeyadej, Mehek Daga

Project Description: This Sankey diagram visualizes how resources flow across categories and helps identify key transitions and bottlenecks.

About the Visualization

This Sankey diagram visualizes the flow of startups through different stages of funding rounds—grouped into 1–3 rounds, 4–6 rounds, and 7+ rounds—and how these stages connect to various funding sources. The diagram reveals that venture capital is the dominant funding source across all stages, particularly in the early rounds.

Startups in their early phases often rely on angel investors, seed funding, and convertible notes. As companies mature through additional funding rounds, they begin to diversify their funding sources, increasingly turning to private equity, debt financing, grants, and post-IPO options. This progression highlights how startups evolve their financial strategies over time to support growth and scalability.

The visualization was created using D3.js and is designed to be clear, accessible, and interactive. The width of each flow represents the volume of funding or transitions, making it easy to identify dominant trends and resource distribution across stages.
